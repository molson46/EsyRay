Hi John,
This has been a very trying time for the Smith Family.  Thursday Jaysen Underhill  flew with J. Martinez to Fredericksburg to show a plane to a possible customer.  He was to come home to go with Michelle to a Wake for her best friend.  Jaysen didn't get home.  Michelle and her sister Barbara kept day and night vigil on the phone for anyone that had any information on the plane that left Thursday morning. She got word of the crash Saturday.  The supposition is that there was a leak and carbon monoxide entered the cabin of the plane and put both the pilot and co-pilot to sleep.  It was on automatic pilot and flew a straight line from Midway Airport between Waxahachie and Midlothian, straight over Fredericksburg airport to crash 1 mile on the other side of the Mexican Border.  Most of the information comes from the Civil Air Patrol.  A member of the CAP came to our son Kevin Joe's house yesterday to give us information on all she knew.  There will be an investigation conducted after the bodies and plane returns to the U.S.
 
Ross Smith

Jaysen left Thursday morning to go to Fredricksburg with the manager of the Midway Airport.  They were friends and had flown together countless times.  A man wanted to sell his plane and they were taking it to Fredricksburg to show a potential buyer.
 
 
Today is just another day for some but it is a day to try healing for the Smiths.  We have heard all the what if's and we have said over and over what if.  All the things that have been heard from the various companies looking during the time Jaysen was missing have been helpful and hopeful to Michelle. Last night Johnnie, son of the man that flew with Jaysen, came over to Joe's.  He went to Piedras Negras to get his father and Jaysen home.  He spoke Spanish fluently and kept the Doctors and, whoever, from doing an autopsy on the bodies.  They were then to be cremated.  This would have not shown that they had carbon monxide in their bodies.  The lethal is 50% (dead) and Jaysen had 70%, knowing this  is comforting to the family, they didn't suffer, but just went to sleep.  They had talked to the airport controllers on the way to Fredrecksburg, their destination.  But sometime before they got there they went to sleep. The plane, on automatic pilot flew on till it ran out of gas and crashed in Mexico.

So many of the air companies, such as the CAP, Government and so many more that I don't remember the names looked for them on this side of the border.  They were found Saturday1 mile from the border in Mexico.
Michelle has had so many phone calls and didn't give up hope till the very end.
We have so many to thank for their help and caring.  Friends and family are what makes us able to get through these rough days.
